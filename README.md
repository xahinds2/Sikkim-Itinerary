## Must-Visit Places in Gangtok & North Sikkim

### Gangtok - Top Attractions

#### Religious & Cultural Sites
- **Rumtek Monastery** (24 km): One of the largest and most significant monasteries in Sikkim, headquarters of the Karma Kagyu lineage
- **Do Drul Chorten Stupa**: Beautiful stupa surrounded by 108 prayer wheels, peaceful atmosphere
- **Enchey Monastery**: 200-year-old monastery with stunning views, hosts the Chaam dance in December/January
- **Ganesh Tok**: Small Ganesh temple with panoramic views of Gangtok and Kanchenjunga
- **Hanuman Tok**: Temple dedicated to Lord Hanuman at 7,200 ft, offers spectacular mountain views

#### Viewpoints & Nature
- **Tashi View Point**: Best spot for sunrise views of Mt. Kanchenjunga
- **MG Marg (Mall Road)**: Heart of Gangtok, pedestrian-only zone with shops, cafes, and restaurants
- **Banjhakri Falls**: Scenic waterfall with beautiful landscaped garden and traditional sculptures
- **Flower Exhibition Centre**: Showcases rare orchids and seasonal flowers native to Sikkim

#### Museums & Institutes
- **Namgyal Institute of Tibetology**: World-renowned center for Tibetan studies, houses rare manuscripts and artifacts
- **Directorate of Handicrafts & Handloom**: Showcases local handicrafts, carpets, and traditional crafts

#### Adventure & Experiences
- **Gangtok Ropeway**: Cable car ride offering bird's-eye views of the city and surrounding valleys
- **Tsomgo Lake (Changu Lake)**: Sacred glacial lake at 12,310 ft, frozen in winter (permit required)
- **Baba Harbhajan Singh Mandir**: Memorial temple near Tsomgo Lake, revered by Army and locals
- **Nathula Pass** (14,140 ft): Indo-China border, requires special permit, closed on Mondays & Tuesdays

### North Sikkim - Must-Visit Places

#### Lachung Area
- **Yumthang Valley** (11,800 ft): "Valley of Flowers" - stunning meadows with hot springs
- **Zero Point** (15,300 ft): Last civilian point before Tibet border, snow-covered terrain year-round
- **Lachung Village**: Traditional Lepcha village, gateway to Yumthang Valley
- **Hot Springs**: Natural hot sulphur springs at Yumthang with therapeutic properties

#### Lachen Area
- **Gurudongmar Lake** (17,800 ft): One of the highest lakes in the world, sacred to Buddhists and Sikhs
- **Chopta Valley**: Scenic high-altitude valley with pristine landscapes
- **Lachen Monastery**: Ancient monastery in traditional Lepcha architecture

#### Other North Sikkim Attractions
- **Seven Sisters Waterfall**: Series of seven waterfalls along the North Sikkim highway
- **Singhik View Point**: Offers majestic views of Mt. Siniolchu and Mt. Khangchendzonga
- **Chungthang**: Confluence of Lachen Chu and Lachung Chu rivers, base for North Sikkim trips

### Best Time to Visit
- **Gangtok**: Year-round destination
  - **March-May**: Pleasant weather, blooming rhododendrons (10-25¬∞C)
  - **October-December**: Clear skies, best mountain views (5-15¬∞C)
  - **December-February**: Cold but less crowded (0-10¬∞C)
- **North Sikkim**: 
  - **Best**: April-June & September-November
  - **Winter (Dec-Feb)**: Very cold, Yumthang covered in snow
  - **Monsoon (July-Sep)**: Avoid due to landslides

### Special Notes
- **Permits Required**: 
  - Tsomgo Lake & Nathula Pass (day permits)
  - North Sikkim (3-7 day permits through registered travel agents)
- **Altitude Considerations**: Acclimatize properly before visiting high-altitude areas
- **Travel Time**: North Sikkim requires minimum 2-3 nights; day trips are exhausting
